The method

How Every Loaf Is Made

Sourdough isn't fast. That's the whole point. Here's what happens between starter and table.

1

Feed the Starter

Beth's 10-year-old starter gets fed every morning. A bubbly, active starter is everything.

2

Mix & Autolyse

Flour and water come together and rest. This builds gluten structure naturally, without kneading.

3

Stretch, Fold & Ferment

Gentle folds every 30 minutes. A long bulk ferment develops flavor and strength.

4

Cold Proof & Bake

Shaped loaves rest overnight in the fridge, then go straight into a screaming-hot dutch oven.

Wisdom from the kitchen

Mama Beth's Tips

Hard-won lessons from a decade of sourdough baking. Keep these close.

🫙

Treat your starter like family

Feed it at the same time every day. Use it at its peak — when it's domed and bubbly, about 4–6 hours after feeding. A well-loved starter is irreplaceable.

🌡️

Temperature, not time

Your dough doesn't know what your recipe says. In summer it rises fast; in winter, slowly. Learn to read the dough, not the clock. 78°F is your sweet spot.

🤲

Stretch-and-fold is your friend

Four rounds of stretch-and-fold during bulk ferment builds strength without overworking the dough. Gentle hands make better bread.

❄️

Cold proof overnight

Shaping and retarding overnight in the refrigerator does two things: builds deep flavor AND makes scoring dramatically easier. Never skip this step.

🔪

Score with conviction

Hold your lame at a 30-degree angle and cut in one swift, confident motion. Hesitation is the enemy of a beautiful ear. Practice on focaccia first.

📓

Keep a baking journal

Write down your flour, hydration, timing, and result after every bake. You'll spot patterns quickly and stop repeating the same mistakes. Mama Beth has filled five notebooks.
